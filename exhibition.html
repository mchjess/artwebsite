Impressions

Impressions shares part of a research-based practice grounded in frottage and automatism, using direct contact with living trees to explore how natural surfaces generate form with and without imposed imagery. These rubbings remain open fields of texture, value, and colour and represent poetic records of encounter rendered with the products traditionally made from trees -- paper and charcoal. The work resists compositional hierarchy and symbolic overlay, allowing bark, pressure, repetition, and duration to determine the visual outcome. In doing so, the project foregrounds slow perception and close looking, inviting viewers into an intimate, sustained relationship with natural patterning that is usually encountered only in passing.
